Proposed structure for a cipher tab

Walk through Caesar as the template — Substitution and Vigenère would follow the same skeleton with their own content. Each numbered block is a scrollable section. Click any section you'd want to drop, or tell me what's missing.

1

Intro — what it is, in one paragraph

"A Caesar cipher shifts every letter by a fixed amount." One worked example shown statically (HELLO → KHOOR with shift 3). No animation yet — just framing.

2

How encryption works — animated shift wheel / strip

Two stacked alphabet strips that slide against each other; click a button to set the shift, watch a sample word transform letter-by-letter. The "key point" picture for the cipher's mechanic.

3

Why it's weak — the keyspace

One sentence + small visual: "There are only 25 possible shifts. You can try them all by hand." A grid showing all 25 candidate decryptions of a short ciphertext.

4

How to crack it — frequency analysis (animated)

The attack story: count letter frequencies in the ciphertext, line them up against English, the shift that matches wins. Small histogram animation showing the cipher histogram sliding into place over the English histogram.

5

"Try it yourself" — link to the Crypto-solver tab

Closing CTA: a button that switches to the Crypto-solver tab with cipher pre-set to Caesar. No embedded solver — the existing tab keeps full control, this is just the doorway.

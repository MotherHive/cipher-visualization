How should the tabs be organized?

You've got 3 ciphers and 3 cracking methods. There are a few ways to split them across tabs.

A

One tab per cipher (cipher + its attack together)

Tabs: Crypto-solver · Caesar · Substitution · Vigenère

Each cipher tab explains how the cipher works and the cracking method that breaks it. The math (frequency analysis, hill climbing, IC) lives where it's first useful. Good for presenting linearly.

B

Ciphers and attacks as separate tabs

Tabs: Crypto-solver · Caesar · Substitution · Vigenère · Frequency Analysis · Hill Climbing · Index of Coincidence

Cleaner conceptual split — the attacks become reusable concepts. More tabs to manage, and you'll repeat yourself when one attack applies to two ciphers.

C

Two top-level sections: "Ciphers" and "Attacks"

Tabs: Crypto-solver · Ciphers · Attacks

Two tabs each containing sub-pages or an accordion. Compact nav bar, but adds a layer of clicking and a small navigation system to build.
